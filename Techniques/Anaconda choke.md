# Anaconda choke

## Used from

- [[Front Headlock / Guillotine Position]]

## How to Identify

The choking arm is threaded under the defender's neck and exits beneath their near-side armpit, with the hand gripping the attacker's own bicep while the free arm clamps over the back in a tight figure-four. The defender is usually turtled or mid-roll, head pinned to the attacker's chest, as the attacker sprawls their hips out and drives shoulder weight down into the trapped neck.
