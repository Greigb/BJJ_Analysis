# Americana

## Used from

- [[Side Control (Top)]]

## How to Identify

From perpendicular, chest-to-chest side control, the top player isolates one of the bottom's arms in a figure-four grip — one hand pins the wrist flat to the mat while the other threads under the tricep to clasp the pinning wrist. The trapped arm is bent at roughly 90 degrees in an 'L' or 'paintbrush' shape, forearm pointing toward the bottom player's head, with the elbow kept on the mat as the wrist is dragged hipward.
