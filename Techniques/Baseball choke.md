# Baseball choke

## Used from

- [[Knee on Belly]]
- [[Side Control (Top)]]

## How to Identify

Both of the attacker's hands grip the collars close together in a baseball-bat configuration — one hand thumb-in deep on one lapel, the other four-fingers-in on the opposite lapel, wrists crossed or stacked at the throat. The forearms pinch into both carotids as the top player rotates hips toward belly-down, driving chest weight through the joined hands.
