## Top Improvements

1. Passing consolidation: You passed guard 2-3 times but couldn't hold side control. After passing, immediately advance to mount or knee on belly — don't stay in side control where the opponent can reguard. Use heavy crossface and hip pressure.
2. Closed guard posture: Got caught in closed guard 3 times and eventually swept from it. Work on standing guard breaks — stand up with one leg posted, break the legs open, then pass from standing. Don't stay kneeling inside closed guard.
3. Bottom game recovery after sweep: Once swept at 2:39, gave up too much position too quickly (side control → mount). From bottom, immediately frame and hip escape. Don't let the top player settle.

## Strengths Observed

- Persistent top pressure: Kept working for passes even after being reguarded multiple times. Good work ethic and pressure on top.
- Guard passing variety: Used multiple passing approaches (pressure pass, headquarters, knee slide). The passing itself is solid — it's the consolidation that needs work.

## Overall Notes

This roll tells a clear story: Greig's top passing game is developing well (the passes themselves are clean) but the missing piece is consolidation — holding dominant position after achieving it. The pattern of pass → reguard → pass → reguard happened 3+ times. The fix is simple but requires drilling: after every pass, immediately advance. Side control is a transition, not a destination. Move to mount, knee on belly, or back take within 3-5 seconds of achieving side control. The second half showed the bottom game needs work — being swept from closed guard is a posture issue that can be fixed with standing guard breaks. Overall a competitive roll with clear, specific areas to improve.
